Who Am I?

It's hard to describe myself within a few generic titles. Some may say I'm a data scientist or entreprenuer. Often times, just to avoid confusion, I'll settle with one of these in conversations. Deep down, though, I'm really just a forever learner.

As a small town kid in Kansas, I didn't know a whole lot. One thing I did have, though, was curiousity. I asked my mother so many questions about nature she probably felt more like the Encyclopaedia Britannica than my mother. By 17 I became interested in the amazing field of mathematics and, shortly after, fell in love. Ever since, mathematics has been a big part of my life and work.

I've also learned that mathematics can do more than help understand problems in science, logic, and philosophy. Paired with data and good programming skills, it can solve real-world problems with high precision. I've worked with startups on science and engineering applications, helped oil companies understand their systems through mathematical models and machine learning, and recently founded AppliedMath.org, an online educational website where I share the power and beauty of mathematics.

Of course, I'm more than just a forever learner. If I'm not sharing my knowledge with others or finding new ways to solve problems with mathematics and data, I'm hiking in the mountains with my amazing wife, reading a good fiction novel, or going for a swim.
