Who Am I?

It's hard to describe myself within a few generic titles. Some may say I'm a data scientist, software developer, or entrepreneur. Often times, just to avoid confusion, I'll settle with one of these in conversations. Deep down, though, I'm really just a forever learner.

As a small-town kid in Kansas, I didn't know a whole lot. One thing I did have, though, was curiosity. I asked my mother so many questions about nature she probably felt more like the Encyclopaedia Britannica than my mother. By seventeen I became interested in the amazing field of mathematics and, shortly after, fell in love. Ever since mathematics has been a big part of my life and work.

While in university, I came across computer science while working on a swarm intelligence research project. Like mathematics, I fell in love with computer science. Having a mixed passion between these fields, I've learned the power of combining them. When paired together, both can solve real-world problems with high precision. This realization has given me many great experiences. As a result, I've worked with startups on science and engineering applications, helped oil companies understand their systems through mathematical models and machine learning, and recently founded Computic.org, an online educational website where I share the application of mathematics in fields like computer science, machine learning, and many more.

Of course, I'm more than just a forever learner. If I'm not sharing my knowledge with others or finding new ways to solve problems, I'm hiking in the mountains with my amazing wife, reading a good fiction novel, or going for a swim.
